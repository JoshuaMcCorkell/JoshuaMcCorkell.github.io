My Interest in IT and How it Started

My main interest in IT is programming. I really enjoy creating computer programs that work (rather than not work!), and I like the process of coding, researching, debugging, etc., especially when the project I’m working on interests me. I’m also taking Introduction to Programming alongside this course.

My interest in IT started when I was in grade 5 (I think) when I bought a book about programming from our school book-fair. The main thing it taught in the book was programming with Scratch, and a bit of Python. I signed up for an account on Scratch, loved it, and the rest is history.

For some years I enjoyed playing around and making programs on Scratch (with a little bit of Python thrown in there). More recently I have enjoyed programming using Python and Java, and also looking into other languages and tools that piqued my interest.

Why I Chose to Come to RMIT

I chose to study through RMIT because I was informed about the online Bachelor of IT program by an Open Universities Australia student advisor. As I have been home-schooled for the past four and a half years, I don't have an ATAR, and I was looking for a way to study IT. Doing the Bachelor of IT through RMIT online seemed like a very good option, so as soon as I was old enough, I enrolled in the two Open-Access Subjects. So far I have found it great, with good quality learning materials and a great community of students and tutors.

What I'm Expecting/Hoping to Learn During My Dtudies

During my studies I hope to learn a lot about IT, and in particular programming. In particular, some of the things I expect to learn are:

  • Useful and modern skills in programming, for example web development, development with Java, Python, and other languages, and general software development principles.
  • Essential skills for other areas of IT, for example cyber and computer security, cloud and database systems etc.
  • Other skills that are useful for having a career in IT (especially in this specific unit), for example how to do group work, and be knowledgeable in important modern IT topics.
